
# Imagine Cup 2026: MVP Stage Requirements & Guidelines

## 1. Core Objective
The goal of the MVP (Minimum Viable Product) stage is to build a functional version of your solution that demonstrates customer validation, market fit, and reflects thoughtful consideration of diversity and inclusion. 
* **functionality:** Your MVP must be ready for a demo to judges and include sufficient features/functionality to satisfy early adopters
* **AI Integration:** The product must use at least two (2) Microsoft AI services (e.g., Azure AI Foundry, Azure Machine Learning) as a core part of the solution.

## 2. Important Dates
* **Submission Deadline:** January 9, 2026 (23:59 UTC).

## 3. Submission Deliverables
To compete, you must submit the following materials by the deadline. All materials must be in English.

### A. Pitch Deck
A slide presentation used to present your startup.
* **Length:** Maximum 15 slides (including appendix).
* **Format:** PPT, PPTX, or PDF.
* **File Size:** Maximum 100MB (ZIP compression allowed).
* **Required Content:**
    * Problem definition.
    * Validation of assumptions with real customers.
    * Solution architecture.
    * Comprehensive list of Microsoft technologies used.

### B. Recorded Videos
You must provide publicly accessible URLs (e.g., OneDrive) for two video files. **Do not password protect** these files.
* **Editing Restrictions:** You may not edit the videos except to trim the beginning/ending.
* **File Size:** Maximum 100MB per video (ZIP compression allowed).

**1. Pitch Video (Max 3 Minutes)**
* Record your team pitching as if presenting live to judges/investors.
* Simulate the experience of a judge viewing your pitch (place camera where a judge would sit).

**2. Demo Video (Max 2 Minutes)**
* A narrated video showing the functional version of the solution.
* Must demonstrate the application of Microsoft AI services and how a user interacts with the product.
* Should mimic a live demo.

### C. Application Form
* Completed responses detailing the team's experience in building technical solutions.

### D. Optional: Interactive Prototypes
* You may share high-fidelity prototypes (e.g., Figma, Axure) to show functionality.
* Must be a direct, publicly available link (no password).

## 4. Judging Criteria (MVP Round)
Your submission will be evaluated based on the following weighted criteria:

### Use of Microsoft Technology (40%)
* **AI Integration:** How meaningfully the team leveraged Microsoft AI services (e.g., Azure AI Studio, Cognitive Services) as core to the value proposition.
* **Robustness:** How effectively the team applied cloud and AI platforms to build a scalable solution.
* **Demonstration:** A technical summary and demo clearly showing Microsoft AI in action.

### Founder Insight, Market Viability, & Inclusive Design (30%)
* **Problem:** Articulation of a real, urgent, and original problem supported by market research.
* **Diversity:** Thoughtful consideration of diversity and inclusion in design and impact.
* **Fit:** Demonstration of unique strengths and founder-market fit.
* **Strategy:** A robust, data-driven go-to-market plan targeting enterprise customers.

### Founder-led Validation & Continuous Improvement (30%)
* **Engagement:** Credible evidence of user/customer engagement.
* **Iteration:** Clear examples of how feedback has informed early iterations of the solution.
